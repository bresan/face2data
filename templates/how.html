How it works

I guess you've probably heard of Artificial Neural Networks, right? Besides the whole hype around this term, that is pretty much what we are using in this project.

By using a large dataset to train (in other words, to teach) a Neural Network how to distinguish between different genders and races, as well as to estimate the age of a given person, we are then able to predict these characteristics on previously unknown faces. Pretty cool, isn't it?

After this brief introduction, if you are interested in still learning about Neural Networks, I highly recommend the /r/learnmachinelearning/ subreddit. Below I'll dive a little bit more into the details of this project, so if you don't understand something, feel free to reach me for some help.


The training phase

Our Neural Network was composed of three major branches, one for each of our features. We have used a default set of hidden layers, based on the stacking of several Conv2D with ReLU activation, followed by a Batch Normalization, then a MaxPooling and finally a Dropout layer. The full Neural Network architecture can be seen below:



We performed our training phase by adopting an Adam optimizer with a learning rate of 1e-4 and a decay based on the initial learning rate divided by the number of epochs.
